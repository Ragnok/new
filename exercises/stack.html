Stacks

Assume that typedef stack stores double and has the following functions defined:

stack *stack_create(void)
void stack_destroy(stack *s)
void stack_push(stack *s, double datum)
double stack_pop(stack *s)
bool stack_is_empty(stack *s)

Implement the following methods:

void stack_reverse(stack **s)
Reverse the stack.
bool stack_is_sorted(stack *s, int (*cmp)(double, double))
Checks to see if the stack is sorted, using cmp. Note that the stack must be put back after it is checked!

Challenge: Determine the Big-O complexity of each function.

Challenge: Implement versions where the data is of type void *.

Suppose that a client performs an intermixed sequence of push and pop operations. The push operations put the integers 0 through 9 in order on to the stack; the pop operations print out the popped value. Which of the following outputs could not occur?

  • 4 3 2 1 0 9 8 7 6 5
  • 4 6 8 7 5 3 2 9 0 1
  • 2 5 6 7 4 8 9 3 1 0
  • 4 3 2 1 0 5 6 7 8 9
  • 1 2 3 4 5 6 9 8 7 0
  • 0 4 6 5 3 8 1 7 2 9
  • 1 4 7 9 8 6 5 3 0 2
  • 2 1 4 3 6 5 8 7 9 0

-27.5

35
